INTERNET OF THINGS(IOT)

What is IOT?

The Internet of Things (IoT) describes the network of physical objects—“things”—that are embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies for the purpose of connecting and exchanging data with other devices and systems over the internet.

History of IOT

-The idea of embedding sensors and intelligence into physical items was initially proposed in the 1980s when some college students decided to alter a Coca-Cola vending machine to allow for remote monitoring of its contents. But technological advancement was slow and cumbersome.

-Computer scientist Kevin Ashton first used the term "Internet of Things" in 1999. Ashton advocated putting radio-frequency identification (RFID) chips on products to follow them through a supply chain while he was employed by Procter & Gamble.

In 2000, LG announced the first smart refrigerator, in 2007 the first iPhone was launched and by 2008, the number of connected devices exceeded the number of people on the planet.

In 2009, Google started testing driverless cars and in 2011, Google’s Nest smart thermostat hit the market, which allowed remote control of central heating.

IOT - ADVANTAGES AND DISADVANTAGES

Challenges of IOT

  • 1. Security: Security is one of the major challenges of IoT. As the number of connected devices increases, the potential for malicious actors to gain access to sensitive data or systems also increases.
  • 2. Data Management: IoT devices generate large amounts of data that needs to be stored, managed, and analyzed.
  • 3. Interoperability: IoT devices must be able to communicate with each other and with other systems.
  • 4. Privacy: IoT devices can collect sensitive data about users, which raises privacy concerns.
  • 5. Cost: IoT devices and systems can be expensive to implement and maintain.
  • 6. Scalability: IoT systems must be able to scale to accommodate the increasing number of connected devices.
How Internet of things work?

The Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of real-world items that have sensors, software, and other technologies built into them so they can gather and share data. These products might range from common household goods to sophisticated machinery. An IoT platform receives the data once it has been collected, stores it, and processes it to produce insights and prompt actions.

Architecture of IOT

  • 1.Sensors/Actuators are the actual objects that interact with the environment to gather data.
  • 2.Device/Gateway Layer: This layer is in charge of providing data storage, processing, and communication capabilities as well as linking the sensors and actuators to the internet
  • 3.Network Layer: This layer provides the communication infrastructure for the IoT system.
  • 4.Platform Layer: This layer provides the software and hardware infrastructure for the IoT system.
  • 5. Application Layer: This layer consists of the applications and services that make use of the data collected by the IoT system.
Devices & Networks

The IoT devices include wireless sensors, software, actuators, computer devices and more. They are attached to a particular object that operates through the internet, enabling the transfer of data among objects or people automatically without human intervention.

IoT devices share the sensor data they collect by connecting to an IoT gateway or other edge device where data is either sent to the cloud to be analyzed or analyzed locally. Sometimes, these devices communicate with other related devices and act on the information they get from one another

Tools & Platforms

An IoT platform is a collection of parts that enables programmers to disperse applications, gather data from a distance, secure communication, and manage sensors. An IoT platform controls device connectivity and enables the creation of new mobile software apps by developers.

IOT CONNECTIVITY PLATFORMS

An IoT Connectivity Platform is used to manage and monitor the communication protocols that connect devices across WiFi, bluetooth, and mobile internet.

IOT DEVICE MANAGEMENT PLATFORMS

IoT Device Management Platforms provide tools for large organizations to monitor, troubleshoot, and update connected devices remotely.

IOT APPLICATION ENABLEMENT PLATFORMS

create and deploy applications that leverage IoT data, whether they're smart home devices or industrial control systems.

IOT ANALYTICS PLATFORMS

IoT Analytics Platforms help organizations gain insight into the data generated by their connected devices.

IOT in Smart Home

The home can be called smart when it has a range of smart devices that you can control remotely by setting them the way you like to automatize house maintenance. They can also be united into one network. For example, the lights can turn on at the moment you enter the home (lamps will know it from the sensors) or your vacuum cleaner can start cleaning every day at 11 AM.

IOT in Smart City

Smart cities use IoT devices such as connected sensors, lights, and meters to collect and analyze data. The cities then use this data to improve infrastructure, public utilities and services, and more. Smart city devices work to make everyday tasks easier and more efficient, while relieving pain points related to public safety, traffic, and environmental issues.

Smart utility meters, Smart Transportation and Smart waste management solutions.
